About Toledo

Below you will find information about Toledo, how to get there, where to stay and what to do during your visit (apart from attending our wedding ;) )

  • Travel

    If you are coming from outside of Spain, the best way to get to Toledo is to fly to Madrid. From there you have several options to get to Toledo:

    Train: This is the best option. The AVANT high-speed trains take you to Toledo in just over half an hour and leave you close to the historic center of the city. There is usually a train every hour. More information.

    Bus: The Alsa company buses leave from Plaza Elíptica and leave you in the historic center of the city in about 60-90 minutes. More information.

    Car: If you prefer to drive, you can get from Madrid to Toledo in an hour (depending on traffic), on the A-42 motorway. Keep in mind that you will probably have to park in a paid car park in Toledo.

  • What to See in Toledo

    Toledo is known as the "City of the Three Cultures" because for centuries Jews, Muslims and Christians coexisted peacefully. It is a city steeped in history and has been declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO. If you have never visited it, it is certainly worth spending a day touring the most important points of the city. Here are some suggestions:

    • Toledo Cathedral: Gothic style built between 1226 and 1493. It's worth going inside.
    • Alcázar de Toledo: It was built as a royal residence and now houses the Army Museum. It is an impressive building that is worth visiting.
    • Monasterio de San Juan de los Reyes: Elizabethan Gothic style monastery, built in 1476 by order of the Catholic Monarchs. Inside you can see its impressive two-story cloister.
    • Mezquita del Cristo de la Luz: This mosque was built in the year 999 as a mosque and is considered the best preserved building from the Muslim era in the city.
    • Sinagoga de Santa María La Blanca: A synagogue built in 1180, it was used for more than 200 years as a synagogue, until it was converted into a church in 1411.
    • Puerta de Bisagra: An arch of Arab origin and which was remodeled during the 16th century. It is located very close to the Hacienda del Cardenal.
    • Plaza del Zocodover: A square and nerve centre of the city. Very close to the Alcazar.
    • Puente de Alcántara: one of the iconic bridges of the city. Historically it served as the entrance to the city on the Tagus River.
    • Puente de San Martín: medieval bridge that gave access to the city.
    • Mirador del Valle: Located south of the city, a Mirador is a sightseeing location, you will have fantastic views of the city and the Tagus River.

    In addition to all this, our wedding coincides with Toledo's Semana Grande (their Big Week), when the Corpus Christi takes place (June 8th). You can see Toledo decked out like never before, as well as attend the special festivities and processions that take place in the city during those days. More information.
